The Di Martino Legacy

In 1912, Giuseppe Di Martino opened a small pasta workshop in Gragnano — a town nestled in the hills south of Naples that had been making pasta since the 16th century. The unique climate, with its warm Mediterranean breeze and mountain air, created the perfect conditions for drying pasta naturally.

Four generations later, the Di Martino family continues this craft with the same dedication. They use only the finest Italian durum wheat, bronze dies that create the signature rough texture, and slow drying over 24-48 hours.

What makes Di Martino special isn't just their history — it's their obsessive attention to detail. Every batch is tasted and tested. The wheat is sourced from specific farms in Puglia and Campania. The bronze dies are maintained by hand. This is pasta-making as an art form.

Their Process

01

Wheat Selection

Only Italian durum wheat from certified farms in Puglia and Basilicata. Each harvest is evaluated for protein content and gluten strength.

02

Bronze Extrusion

Dough is pressed through traditional bronze dies — never Teflon. This creates a rough, porous surface that grips sauce beautifully.

03

Slow Drying

Pasta dries naturally for 24-48 hours in climate-controlled rooms that mimic Gragnano's historic drying conditions. Low temperature preserves flavor.

04

Quality Control

Every batch is cooked and tasted by the Di Martino family. If it doesn't meet their standards, it doesn't leave the factory.
