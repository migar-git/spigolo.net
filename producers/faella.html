A Family Obsession

The Faella family has been making pasta in Gragnano since 1870. Walk into their factory today, and you'll find many of the same bronze dies and drying techniques used over a century ago.

What sets Faella apart is their uncompromising approach to slow drying. While most artisan producers dry for 24-36 hours, Faella extends this to 36-48 hours for long shapes and up to 72 hours for thick formats like paccheri and rigatoni.

The result? Pasta with extraordinary depth of flavor and a texture that's simultaneously tender and toothsome. It's why Michelin-starred chefs across Italy — and the world — insist on Faella.

Their paccheri, in particular, is legendary. These giant tube-shaped pastas are perfect for seafood sauces, maintaining their structure while drinking in every drop of flavor.
