The Ancient Grain Revolution

Gentile stands out among Gragnano's pasta makers for their pioneering work with ancient grain varieties. While other producers focus on modern durum wheat, the Gentile family has spent decades perfecting pasta made from heritage wheats like Senatore Cappelli, Timilia, and Russello.

These ancient varieties were nearly lost to history, replaced by high-yield modern strains. But the Gentile family recognized that these old wheats had something special: deeper flavor, better digestibility, and a connection to centuries of Italian agricultural tradition.

Their fusilli lunghi — long spiral pasta — showcases this beautifully. The bronze-die extrusion creates deep grooves that capture sauce, while the heritage wheat adds a nutty, complex flavor you won't find in conventional pasta.

Four generations of the Gentile family work side-by-side in their Gragnano workshop, passing down techniques that can't be learned from books. This is pasta-making as a living tradition.

Heritage Wheat Varieties

Senatore Cappelli

A durum wheat variety from the early 1900s, known for its high protein content and rich, golden color. Nutty flavor with hints of hazelnut.

Timilia

Ancient Sicilian wheat with a dark, rustic appearance. Earthy flavor profile, lower gluten, exceptional for traditional shapes.

Russello

Rare Sicilian variety prized for its sweet, delicate flavor. Difficult to work with, but the results are extraordinary.
