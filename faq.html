Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ordering and enjoying our pasta

What makes your pasta different from supermarket pasta?

Our pasta is made by small, family-run producers using traditional methods: bronze-die extrusion (not Teflon), slow drying over 24-72 hours (not 2 hours), and premium Italian wheat. The result is pasta with superior texture, deeper flavor, and better sauce adhesion. It's the difference between fast fashion and haute couture.

Where does your pasta come from?

All our pasta is made in Italy by multi-generational family businesses. Most of our producers are in Gragnano (Campania) — the birthplace of dried pasta — with others from Tuscany, Abruzzo, and Puglia.

How should I store the pasta?

Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. Keep sealed in the original packaging or transfer to an airtight container. Dried pasta has a shelf life of 2-3 years, but we recommend using within 12 months for peak flavor.

How do I cook it differently than regular pasta?

The technique is the same, but artisan pasta often cooks faster than mass-market pasta — especially thin shapes. Start tasting 2-3 minutes before the package time. Use plenty of salted water, reserve some pasta water for finishing, and always finish cooking the pasta in the sauce. Check out our guide to cooking perfect pasta.

Do you ship internationally?

Currently we ship within the United States only. International shipping is coming soon! Join our newsletter to be notified when we expand.

How fast do you ship?

Orders placed before 2 PM EST ship the same day. Standard shipping (3-5 business days) is free on orders over $50. Expedited 2-day shipping is available for $12.

Can I return pasta if I don't like it?

We offer a 100% satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with your purchase, contact us within 30 days for a full refund or exchange. We'll even let you keep the pasta — just tell us what you didn't like so we can improve.

Are your products organic?

Some are, some aren't. We prioritize quality and tradition over certifications. Many of our producers use organic or sustainably grown wheat even if they don't pursue certification. Product pages indicate which items are certified organic.

Is the pasta suitable for people with dietary restrictions?

Our pasta is made from durum wheat (contains gluten) and occasionally eggs (for fresh pasta). It is NOT suitable for gluten-free or vegan diets. We may add gluten-free options in the future.

Why is it so expensive?

Great pasta isn't cheap to make. Our producers use premium wheat, traditional bronze dies (which wear out and must be replaced), and slow drying (which ties up production capacity for days). They're small-batch producers, not industrial manufacturers. The difference in taste and texture is worth it — think of it as the difference between craft beer and Budweiser.

Absolutely! Our curated gift boxes are perfect for food lovers. All gift boxes include beautiful packaging, tasting notes, and the option to add a personalized message at checkout.
