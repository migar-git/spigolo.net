About Spigolo

Founded by pasta obsessives who believe the world deserves better than supermarket spaghetti

It Started with a Meal

Three years ago, we traveled to Gragnano, Italy — the small town south of Naples known as the birthplace of dried pasta. We'd eaten plenty of "artisan" pasta back home, but nothing prepared us for what we tasted that evening at a tiny trattoria.

The spaghetti was rough-textured, almost rustic. It had a wheaty, nutty flavor we'd never encountered. The sauce — a simple tomato with basil — clung to every strand. We asked the chef where he got his pasta.

"From Faella," he said, gesturing down the street. "They've been making it the same way for 150 years."

We visited the workshop the next morning. Watched the dough being extruded through ancient bronze dies. Saw the pasta drying slowly in temperature-controlled rooms. Tasted samples that were still warm. We were hooked.

Our Mission

Spigolo exists to bring the world's finest artisan pasta to food lovers who care about quality, tradition, and taste. We work exclusively with multi-generational family producers who:

  • Use bronze dies — never Teflon — for superior texture
  • Slow-dry their pasta over 24-72 hours at low temperatures
  • Source premium wheat — Italian durum or heritage varieties
  • Maintain family traditions passed down through generations
  • Never compromise on quality for mass production

We believe pasta can be a luxury product — not because of fancy packaging, but because of the care, skill, and time that goes into making it. This is the Dior of dried pasta. Only the best.

Why "Spigolo"?

In Italian, "spigolo" means "edge" or "corner" — that precise point where two surfaces meet. It's a word that captures both sharpness and craftsmanship.

Great pasta has edges. The rough texture from bronze-die extrusion creates countless tiny edges that grip sauce. The flavor has edge — nutty, wheaty, complex. And our curation has edge — we're uncompromising about quality.

Plus, we liked that it sounds cool and nobody else had taken it. Sometimes branding is that simple.
